This [study](https://doi.org/10.22541/essoar.176365939.97870596/v1), led by Judith Berner, examines the fundamental limits of subseasonal-to-seasonal weather predictability and the role of land and ocean initial conditions. Using a climate model in a perfect-model framework, the authors show that beyond four weeks, **land surface initialization - particularly soil moisture and snow - dominates predictability over land**, with ocean conditions playing a secondary role.
